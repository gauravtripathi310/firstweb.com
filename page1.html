History of Kushinagar

Nestled in the heart of Uttar Pradesh, Kushinagar is one of the ancient towns that attract people from all over for its beautiful Stupas and Temples. It is believed that Lord Buddha attained salvation in Kushinagar in Uttar Pradesh in India. Indianholiday.com offers online information on History of Kushinagar, Uttar Pradesh and other tourist attractions in Uttar Pradesh and other parts of India. The History of Kushinagar, Uttar Pradesh can be traced from the pre Buddha period. The capital of the Malla kingdom, Kushinagar was often referred as Kushawati or Kushinara. It was one of the mahajanapdas in the 6th century B.C. The Ramayana states that Mallas was a part of the Kosala region in India. According to legends, it was named after Lord Rama's son Kush while according to some it is the abundance of Kush grass that led to its unique name. Kushinagar was an important part of the Maurya, Shunga, Kushana, Gupta and Harsha kingdoms. The History of Kushinagar, Uttar Pradesh states importance of these rulers in the art and architecture of the era. One of the major tourist attractions of Uttar Pradesh, Kushinagar was thought to be one of the flourishing capitals till the 12th century A.D. But then it somehow found its place in the pages of history books. During the 19th century excavations unearthed this city along with its famous Stupas and a 6 metre high statue of Lord Buddha. It is believed that Lord Buddha attained his salvation or Mahaparnirvana in Kushinagar. There is a Mahaparnirvana temple in Kushinagar. On your tour to Kushinagar you can get a glimpse of this temple. Associated with Lord Buddha to Mahavir, the History of Kushinagar, Uttar Pradesh is quite interesting. The beautifully designed Stupas will give you an idea about the History of Kushinagar, Uttar Pradesh. From the Stupas created by the Mallas to the Stucco statues of Lord Buddha, History of Kushinagar, Uttar Pradesh extends to an ancient period. History of Kushinagar, Uttar Pradesh also gives details that it was an important center for Vaishnavism, Shaivism and Jain philosophies. Near the Gandak River it's an ideal place for meditation. There are a number of tourist attractions in Kushinagar which you can explore during your tour to Kushinagar, Uttar Pradesh. There is a tall statue of Lord Buddha near the Nirvana Temple. The archaeologists discovered a black stone image of Lord Buddha which was called the 'Bhumi Sparsha Mudra'. The last sermon of Lord Buddha was delivered at this place. The Watt Thai Temple is one of the most visited temples in Kushinagar. The Japanese temple is also one of the beautiful temples in Uttar Pradesh. There is a Chinese Temple also which attracts the devotees for its distinct and exquisite architecture.
